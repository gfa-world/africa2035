By God's grace, we are in the process of building a world-class hospital and medical University in Kigali, Rwanda, that will become a base to train and send thousands of medical professionals, advance medical research—and transform communities in need throughout Africa, all in the name of Jesus Christ.

You can be part of this unprecedented opportunity to bring life-saving healing and hope to the people of Africa.

Planned Medical Complex

The Hospital

Completion Date
April 2026
Estimated Cost
$40 million

The hospital will have 300 beds and state-of-the-art equipment to serve the health care needs of people in the immediate region and beyond.

It will include 12 to 14 specialist departments, including cardiology, neurology and intensive care units.

We estimate serving 500,000+ outpatients in the first 2 years of operations.

Our facility is situated just six miles from the capital, Kigali, where nearly 900,000 people live. It will be the second major hospital in a country with a population of more than 14 million and only about 1,500 doctors. As we employ hundreds of medical professionals—and train hundreds more every year—we aim to reduce that disproportionate ratio so quality health care can be available to all people who need it.

This hospital will be Christ-centered and holistic, offering both patients and their families prayer, Bibles and a place to seek the Lord at the onsite chapel.

The Medical University & Staffing Quarters

Target Completion Date
August 2026
Estimated Cost
$20 million

The medical university will welcome up to 100 students each year, multiplying the hospital's impact by actively training and attracting doctors from Rwanda and other African nations. It will create a steady flow of new professionals to ensure this important work continues far into the future.

Everyone who attends BIHMU for medical education will be given a strong understanding of underserved areas and the underprivileged. They'll have opportunities for practical training on the field, helping with medical camps and gaining real-world experience.

The medical university is a catalyst to long-term service and ministry throughout the continent. We expect to recruit students from across Africa who have a passion and desire to use their training to serve those most in need within their home communities.

Upon graduation, they'll be equipped to start medical clinics, helping under-resourced communities. And right alongside, will be opportunities to express the compassionate heart of Christ. Through this extension of ministry, new churches can be established, extending healing and hope to all who come.

The Staffing Quarters is crucial to the development of the hospital and medical university. It will allow for international medical professionals to live near the hospital and university for prolonged periods while we train health workers to take over the work. Our desire is, through our medical university, to provide a source of medical professionals with the field of expertise required for their own nations' particular needs and growing population. Through this endeavor, we are providing the rising generation with the resources they need for their continent to thrive.

Research and Development Centre

Target Completion Date
2028
Estimated Cost
$15 million

The new Research and Development Centre will encompass the specialized sectors of medicine, offering high-end services and expert-level care in areas such as oncology, transplantation, and more.

This cutting-edge Research and Development Centre will also help bring major research and medical discoveries impacting local populations who will benefit the most. They won't have to go looking outside their own borders to find help and healing because we'll be able to do the researching for them.

The Need in Africa is Vast

  • 375,000 people in Africa die each year from malaria, a disease that can be prevented and cured if treated promptly
  • Less than 2% of the medicine used in Africa is produced domestically—98% must be imported
  • More than 50% of African children under 15 suffer from starvation, sickness and poverty

Furthermore, millions of people on this continent have never heard even one time that God loves them. Eleven of the world's least-evangelized groups of people are in Africa. It is estimated that more than 160 million people here are still waiting to hear the Good News.

We're already providing care to the people of Rwanda. This hospital will amplify it.

Going Forward

The establishment of the hospital and medical university in Rwanda will provide the foundation for transformational missions work to begin throughout Africa. We have long been committed to reaching those without hope in this world and the next with the love of Christ in word and deed. And as BIHMU becomes more and more self-sustaining, it will be able to directly enable community transformation projects and compassion-driven ministry around the continent.

The hospital in Rwanda will be a tremendous resource beyond the borders of the country for those who will travel to the hospital from other countries, as well as through the use of telemedicine.

We believe that through this endeavor the Lord will provide ongoing relationships with communities that, otherwise, we may never have been able to connect with. The opportunity to purposefully extend Christ's compassion and hope to areas in need is unprecedented.

Following Christ's example of compassion, we aim to provide healing for body, soul and spirit. Wherever and however we administer compassion, we also administer God's love.

Timeline

2014
Believers Church Medical University Hospital established in South India.
2019
Senior church leaders from Rwanda approached us, asking if it was possible to build a similar, much-needed hospital in their home country.
2021
God moved on the heart of a supporter to provide the resources necessary to purchase the land where the medical hospital would be built. Official groundbreaking to begin construction of the hospital
2025
Phase I of the Believers International Hospital is nearing completion.
2026
Phase II construction will begin on the medical University, staffing quarters and campus facilities. Anticipated inauguration of the hospital in May 2026

New Bugesera International Airport will open near our hospital. This will accommodate 7 million passengers annually, allowing Rwanda to become the medical and business hub for Central and Eastern Africa.
2028
All three phases of construction completed: hospital, medical University, staffing quarters and advanced treatment centre.
2035
Expansion efforts will begin for another hospital and medical University in the western region of Africa (in addition to work in Asia).
